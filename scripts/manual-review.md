# Hadith Review — 99 Names of Allah

Review each entry. Cross-check hadiths at **sunnah.com**.

---

## 1. Ar-Rahman — الرَّحْمَنُ

**Quran mentions:** 57  
**Quran refs:** Opens 113 of 114 surahs (Bismillah). The only exception is Surah At-Tawbah (9), which has no Bismillah. Named entirely in Surah Ar-Rahman (55). Also prominent in Al-Fatiha (1:3).  

**Hadith:**  
> "Allah is more merciful to His servants than a mother is to her child." — Bukhari & Muslim

---

## 2. Ar-Raheem — ٱلْرَّحِيْمُ

**Quran mentions:** 95  
**Quran refs:** Mentioned 95 times across the Quran. Pairs with Ar-Rahman in Al-Fatiha (1:3) and throughout the Quran. Surah Al-Baqarah (2:143, 2:163) and countless other surahs.  

**Hadith:**  
> "Verily Allah has one hundred mercies, of which He sent down one between jinn, humans, cattle and all animals... And He retained ninety-nine mercies to show mercy to His servants on the Day of Resurrection." — Muslim

---

## 3. Al-Malik — ٱلْمَلِكُ

**Quran mentions:** 5  
**Quran refs:** Surah Al-Hashr (59:23), Al-Jumu'ah (62:1), Al-Mu'minun (23:116). The majestic Ayat al-Kursi (2:255) speaks of His unlimited sovereignty.  

**Hadith:**  
> "The most disgraceful name in Allah's sight on the Day of Judgment will be that of a man calling himself King of Kings." — Bukhari & Muslim

---

## 4. Al-Quddus — ٱلْقُدُّوسُ

**Quran mentions:** 2  
**Quran refs:** Surah Al-Hashr (59:23) and Al-Jumu'ah (62:1) — always paired with Al-Malik, emphasizing that His kingship is pure and untainted.  

**Hadith:**  
> "Subboohun Quddoosun, Rabb ul-malaa'ikati war-rooh" (Pure, Holy, Lord of the angels and the Spirit) — recited by the Prophet in his bowing and prostration. — Muslim

---

## 5. As-Salam — ٱلْسَّلَامُ

**Quran mentions:** 1  
**Quran refs:** Surah Al-Hashr (59:23). Also referenced in Surah Al-An'am (6:54) where Allah greets believers with peace.  

**Hadith:**  
> "As-Salam is among the names of Allah. Spread it (the greeting of Salam) among yourselves." — Bukhari

---

## 6. Al-Mu'min — ٱلْمُؤْمِنُ

**Quran mentions:** 1  
**Quran refs:** Surah Al-Hashr (59:23) — appears in a powerful sequence of divine names. The root appears throughout the Quran in discussions of faith and security.  

**Hadith:**  
> "None of you truly believes until I am more beloved to him than his own son, his father, and all of mankind." — Bukhari (from Anas ibn Malik). The Prophet ﷺ is the living embodiment of Al-Mu'min's gift of faith — loving him is the mark of true belief.

---

## 7. Al-Muhaymin — ٱلْمُهَيْمِنُ

**Quran mentions:** 1  
**Quran refs:** Surah Al-Hashr (59:23). Also Al-Baqarah (2:257): 'Allah is the Guardian (Waliyy) of those who believe' — the root of guardianship runs throughout. Al-Ma'idah (5:48) uses this root to describe the Quran's guardianship over earlier scriptures.  

**Hadith:**  
> "Be mindful of Allah and He will protect you. Be mindful of Allah and you will find Him before you. When you ask, ask Allah; when you seek help, seek it from Allah." — Tirmidhi (authenticated from Ibn Abbas)

---

## 8. Al-Aziz — ٱلْعَزِيزُ

**Quran mentions:** 94  
**Quran refs:** One of the most frequently mentioned names — 94 times. Often paired with Al-Hakeem (the Wise) and Al-Raheem. Prominent in Surah Al-Baqarah, Al-Imran, and throughout.  

**Hadith:**  
> "O Allah, none can withhold what You give, and none can give what You withhold, and the might of the mighty person cannot benefit him against You." — Bukhari & Muslim

---

## 9. Al-Jabbar — ٱلْجَبَّارُ

**Quran mentions:** 1  
**Quran refs:** Surah Al-Hashr (59:23). The word jabbar is also used in the Quran to describe tyrannical humans — a stark contrast to how it applies to Allah.  

**Hadith:**  
> "Exalted are You, O Jabbar, O Mutakabbir" — part of prophetic supplications recited in night prayer. — Abu Dawud

---

## 10. Al-Mutakabbir — ٱلْمُتَكَبِّرُ

**Quran mentions:** 1  
**Quran refs:** Surah Al-Hashr (59:23) — the final name in a majestic sequence of divine attributes.  

**Hadith:**  
> "Greatness is My cloak and pride is My garment. Whoever contends with Me in either of them, I will punish." — Muslim (Hadith Qudsi)

---

## 11. Al-Khaaliq — ٱلْخَالِقُ

**Quran mentions:** 8  
**Quran refs:** Surah Al-Hashr (59:24), Al-Hijr (15:28), Az-Zumar (39:62), and many others. The act of creation is one of the most discussed divine attributes in the Quran.  

**Hadith:**  
> "Allah was, and there was nothing before Him. His Throne was upon the water. Then He created the heavens and the earth and wrote all things in the Book." — Bukhari (from Imran ibn Husayn). The first act of Al-Khaliq recorded in hadith.

---

## 12. Al-Baari' — ٱلْبَارِئُ

**Quran mentions:** 2  
**Quran refs:** Surah Al-Hashr (59:24) — appears alongside Al-Khaliq and Al-Musawwir, forming a trinity of creative acts: origination, differentiation, and formation.  

**Hadith:**  
> "Each of you is formed in your mother's womb for forty days as a drop, then as a clot for the same period, then as a lump of flesh. Then an angel is sent to breathe the soul into it and inscribe four things: provision, lifespan, deeds, and whether wretched or blessed." — Bukhari & Muslim. Al-Bari' differentiates every soul from the very beginning.

---

## 13. Al-Musawwir — ٱلْمُصَوِّرُ

**Quran mentions:** 1  
**Quran refs:** Surah Al-Hashr (59:24). Also Al-Imran (3:6): 'He is the One who forms you in the womb however He wills.'  

**Hadith:**  
> "Allah fashions the creation in the womb however He wills." — Bukhari & Muslim

---

## 14. Al-Ghaffaar — ٱلْغَفَّارُ

**Quran mentions:** 5  
**Quran refs:** Surah Ta-Ha (20:82): 'And I am the Most Forgiving to whoever repents.' Also Surah Az-Zumar (39:53), Al-Ghafir (40:3).  

**Hadith:**  
> "O My servants, you sin by night and by day and I forgive all sins, so seek forgiveness from Me and I will forgive you." — Muslim (Hadith Qudsi)

---

## 15. Al-Qahhaar — ٱلْقَهَّارُ

**Quran mentions:** 6  
**Quran refs:** Surah Yusuf (12:39): 'Are separate lords better, or Allah — the One, Al-Qahhar?' Also Al-Ra'd (13:16), Ibrahim (14:48), Az-Zumar (39:4).  

**Hadith:**  
> "On the Day of Resurrection, the earth will be turned into a single loaf of bread — the Almighty will knead it with His hand as one of you kneads dough for a journey. Then He will call to the inhabitants of Paradise: I am the King." — Bukhari & Muslim. Al-Qahhar subdues all of creation equally on that Day.

---

## 16. Al-Wahhaab — ٱلْوَهَّابُ

**Quran mentions:** 3  
**Quran refs:** Surah Al-Imran (3:8): 'Our Lord, do not let our hearts deviate after You have guided us. Grant us Your mercy — You are Al-Wahhab.' Also Sad (38:9, 38:35).  

**Hadith:**  
> "Ask Allah for His bounty, for Allah loves to be asked." — Tirmidhi

---

## 17. Ar-Razzaaq — ٱلْرَّزَّاقُ

**Quran mentions:** 1  
**Quran refs:** Surah Adh-Dhariyat (51:58): 'Indeed Allah is Ar-Razzaq, the Owner of Strength, the Firm.' The concept of rizq (provision) appears hundreds of times in the Quran.  

**Hadith:**  
> "If you relied on Allah as He should be relied upon, He would provide for you as He provides for the birds — they leave hungry and return full." — Tirmidhi

---

## 18. Al-Fattaah — ٱلْفَتَّاحُ

**Quran mentions:** 1  
**Quran refs:** Surah Saba (34:26): 'Say: Our Lord will bring us together, then He will judge between us in truth — and He is Al-Fattah, Al-Aleem.' Also Al-Fath (48:1-3).  

**Hadith:**  
> "O Allah, You are Al-Fattah, Al-Aleem" — part of the prophetic dua recited when facing difficulty. — Abu Dawud

---

## 19. Al-Aleem — ٱلْعَلِيمُ

**Quran mentions:** 140  
**Quran refs:** The most frequently paired attribute in the Quran — appearing 140+ times. Surah Al-Baqarah (2:29), Al-An'am (6:59), Luqman (31:34), and throughout every juz.  

**Hadith:**  
> "O Allah, I ask You for beneficial knowledge, good provision, and accepted deeds." — Ibn Majah (authenticated). The Prophet ﷺ recited this every morning, turning to Al-Aleem for knowledge that truly benefits.

---

## 20. Al-Qaabidh — ٱلْقَابِضُ

**Quran mentions:** 1  
**Quran refs:** Al-Baqarah (2:245): 'Allah constricts and expands, and to Him you will be returned.' Al-Qabidh always appears in the Quran alongside Al-Basit.  

**Hadith:**  
> "The hearts of the children of Adam are between the fingers of Ar-Rahman — He contracts and expands them as He wills." — Muslim

---

## 21. Al-Baasit — ٱلْبَاسِطُ

**Quran mentions:** 1  
**Quran refs:** Al-Baqarah (2:245): 'Allah constricts and expands, and to Him you will be returned.' Always appears with Al-Qabidh as a pair.  

**Hadith:**  
> "O Allah, expand for us and do not constrict, and bring it to completion with good." — A prophetic dua upon entering a place. — Abu Dawud

---

## 22. Al-Khaafidh — ٱلْخَافِضُ

**Quran mentions:** ?  
**Quran refs:** Not mentioned by this name in the Quran, but the concept of Allah lowering the arrogant and the enemies of truth is pervasive throughout. This name comes from the Hadith of Tirmidhi listing the 99 names.  

**Hadith:**  
> "Whoever has even an atom's weight of arrogance in his heart will not enter Paradise." A man said: 'What if a man likes to have good clothes and good shoes?' He said: 'Allah is beautiful and loves beauty. Arrogance is rejecting the truth and looking down on people.' — Muslim. Al-Khafidh abases the arrogant.

---

## 23. Ar-Raafi' — ٱلْرَّافِعُ

**Quran mentions:** ?  
**Quran refs:** Not mentioned by this exact name but the concept permeates the Quran: Al-Mujadila (58:11): 'Allah raises those who believe among you and those given knowledge, in degrees.'  

**Hadith:**  
> "Whoever is humble for Allah, Allah will raise him — so that he considers himself small while people consider him great." — Ibn Majah

---

## 24. Al-Mu'izz — ٱلْمُعِزُّ

**Quran mentions:** ?  
**Quran refs:** Al-Imran (3:26): 'You give honor to whom You will and You humiliate whom You will.' This name comes from the Tirmidhi Hadith listing the 99 names.  

**Hadith:**  
> "Indeed Allah raises peoples through this Book and degrades others by it." — Muslim (from 'Umar ibn al-Khattab, who narrated it from the Prophet ﷺ). Al-Mu'izz bestows honor through the Quran — the highest honor is closeness to His word.

---

## 25. Al-Muzil — ٱلْمُذِلُّ

**Quran mentions:** ?  
**Quran refs:** Al-Imran (3:26): 'You give honor to whom You will and You humiliate whom You will.' Paired with Al-Mu'izz in the same verse.  

**Hadith:**  
> "Whoever opposes Allah, Allah will humiliate him." — Sahih al-Jami'

---

## 26. As-Samee' — ٱلْسَّمِيعُ

**Quran mentions:** 45  
**Quran refs:** 45 times in the Quran. Paired constantly with Al-Basir (the Seeing). Surah Al-Mujadilah (58:1) was revealed when Allah heard the plea of Khawla bint Tha'laba. Surah Al-Isra (17:1), Al-Hajj (22:61, 22:75), Al-Hujurat (49:18).  

**Hadith:**  
> 'Aisha said: "Glory be to the One whose hearing encompasses all things. I heard some of what Khawla said but some was hidden from me — and yet Allah heard her complaint from above seven heavens." — Bukhari (context of Surah Al-Mujadilah). As-Sami' hears what even those present cannot.

---

## 27. Al-Baseer — ٱلْبَصِيرُ

**Quran mentions:** 42  
**Quran refs:** 42 times in the Quran. Paired constantly with As-Sami'. Surah Al-Isra (17:1), Al-Hajj (22:61, 22:75), Al-Hujurat (49:18).  

**Hadith:**  
> "Allah does not look at your bodies or your wealth. He looks at your hearts and your deeds." — Muslim (from Abu Hurayrah). Al-Basir's sight penetrates to what is invisible to others — the inner reality of the heart.

---

## 28. Al-Hakam — ٱلْحَكَمُ

**Quran mentions:** 1  
**Quran refs:** Surah Al-An'am (6:114): 'Shall I seek other than Allah as a judge?' The concept of divine judgment pervades the Quran. Al-Hakam appears in the Tirmidhi hadith of names.  

**Hadith:**  
> "Verily, Allah is Al-Hakam and to Him belongs judgment." — Abu Dawud, Nasai

---

## 29. Al-'Adl — ٱلْعَدْلُ

**Quran mentions:** ?  
**Quran refs:** The Quran commands justice in Al-Nahl (16:90) and Al-Ma'idah (5:8). While Al-Adl as a name comes from Hadith, divine justice is central to nearly every Quranic discussion.  

**Hadith:**  
> "Beware of injustice, for injustice will be darkness upon darkness on the Day of Resurrection." — Muslim (from Jabir ibn Abdullah). Al-Adl guarantees that every injustice is accounted for.

---

## 30. Al-Lateef — ٱلْلَّطِيفُ

**Quran mentions:** 7  
**Quran refs:** Surah Al-An'am (6:103): 'Vision cannot encompass Him but He encompasses all vision, and He is Al-Latif, Al-Khabir.' Also Surah Yusuf (12:100) and Al-Hajj (22:63).  

**Hadith:**  
> "My servant does not draw near to Me with anything more beloved to Me than what I have made obligatory on him. My servant continues drawing near to Me with voluntary acts until I love him. When I love him, I am his hearing with which he hears, his sight with which he sees, his hand with which he strikes, and his foot with which he walks." — Bukhari (Hadith Qudsi). Al-Latif works through the subtlest channels when He loves a servant.

---

## 31. Al-Khabeer — ٱلْخَبِيرُ

**Quran mentions:** 45  
**Quran refs:** 45 times in the Quran, often paired with Al-Aleem and Al-Latif. Surah Al-An'am (6:18): 'He is the Subtle, the Acquainted.' Luqman (31:34).  

**Hadith:**  
> "Surely Allah does not look at your forms or your wealth, but He looks at your hearts and your deeds." — Muslim (from Abu Hurayrah). Al-Khabir sees the inner reality — what matters to Him is invisible to people.

---

## 32. Al-Haleem — ٱلْحَلِيمُ

**Quran mentions:** 15  
**Quran refs:** 15 times in the Quran. Often paired with Al-Ghafur and Al-Shakur. Surah Al-Baqarah (2:225, 2:235), Al-Imran (3:155), Al-Nisa (4:12).  

**Hadith:**  
> "Allah extends His hand at night to accept the repentance of the sinner by day, and extends His hand by day to accept the repentance of the sinner by night — until the sun rises from the west." — Muslim. Al-Haleem's patience in accepting repentance has no limit.

---

## 33. Al-Azeem — ٱلْعَظِيمُ

**Quran mentions:** 7  
**Quran refs:** 7 times. Most famously in Ayat al-Kursi (2:255): 'He is the Most High, Al-Azeem.' Also Al-Baqarah (2:255), Al-Shu'ara (26:192), Al-Waqi'ah (56:74, 56:96).  

**Hadith:**  
> "The greatest verse in the Quran is Ayat al-Kursi" — Bukhari. Its conclusion 'wa Huwal Aliyyul Azeem' anchors all divine greatness.

---

## 34. Al-Ghafoor — ٱلْغَفُورُ

**Quran mentions:** 91  
**Quran refs:** 91 times — one of the most frequent divine names. Surah Az-Zumar (39:53): 'Do not despair of Allah's mercy — indeed Allah forgives all sins.' Al-Baqarah (2:173) and throughout.  

**Hadith:**  
> "All the children of Adam are sinners, and the best of sinners are those who repent." — Tirmidhi & Ibn Majah

---

## 35. Ash-Shakoor — ٱلْشَّكُورُ

**Quran mentions:** 4  
**Quran refs:** 4 times. Surah Fatir (35:30, 35:34): 'He is the Forgiving, the Appreciative.' Surah Al-Baqarah (2:158), Al-Taghabun (64:17).  

**Hadith:**  
> "Whoever does a good deed will have it multiplied tenfold up to seven hundred times, and Allah multiplies for whoever He wills." — Muslim

---

## 36. Al-Aliyy — ٱلْعَلِيُّ

**Quran mentions:** 8  
**Quran refs:** 8 times. Ayat al-Kursi (2:255): 'He is Al-Aliyy, Al-Azeem.' Al-Nisa (4:34), Al-Hajj (22:62), Al-Shu'ara (42:51).  

**Hadith:**  
> "Our Lord descends to the lowest heaven every last third of the night." — Bukhari & Muslim. His descent does not diminish His supreme elevation.

---

## 37. Al-Kabeer — ٱلْكَبِيرُ

**Quran mentions:** 6  
**Quran refs:** 6 times. Surah Al-Ra'd (13:9): 'Al-Kabeer, Al-Muta'al.' Al-Nisa (4:34), Al-Hajj (22:62), Saba (34:23).  

**Hadith:**  
> "The most beloved words to Allah are four: Subhanallah, Alhamdulillah, La ilaha illallah, Allahu Akbar. It does not matter with which one you begin." — Muslim (from Samura ibn Jundub). Allahu Akbar — Allah is greater — is the constant proclamation of Al-Kabeer's supremacy.

---

## 38. Al-Hafeez — ٱلْحَفِيظُ

**Quran mentions:** 3  
**Quran refs:** Surah Hud (11:57): 'My Lord is Hafeez over all things.' Surah Yusuf (12:64): 'Allah is the best of guardians.' The preservation of the Quran itself is a manifestation of this name.  

**Hadith:**  
> "Guard Allah (His rights) and He will guard you." — Tirmidhi

---

## 39. Al-Muqeet — ٱلْمُقِيتُ

**Quran mentions:** 1  
**Quran refs:** Surah An-Nisa (4:85): 'And Allah is Muqeet over all things.' This is the only Quranic occurrence of this name.  

**Hadith:**  
> "Allah the Most High says: 'O son of Adam, devote yourself to My worship and I will fill your heart with richness and relieve your poverty. And if you do not, I will fill your hands with busyness and not relieve your poverty.'" — Tirmidhi & Ibn Majah (authenticated). Al-Muqeet provides the precise sustenance — spiritual and material — each soul needs.

---

## 40. Al-Haseeb — ٱلْحَسِيبُ

**Quran mentions:** 3  
**Quran refs:** 3 times. An-Nisa (4:6, 4:86): 'And Allah is sufficient as Reckoner.' Al-Ahzab (33:39): 'And sufficient is Allah as Reckoner.'  

**Hadith:**  
> "On the Day of Judgment, the two feet of the son of Adam will not move until he is asked about five things: his life and how he spent it; his youth and how he used it; his wealth and how he earned it and spent it; and what he did with his knowledge." — Tirmidhi (authenticated). Al-Haseeb's reckoning is precise and comprehensive.

---

## 41. Al-Jaleel — ٱلْجَلِيلُ

**Quran mentions:** ?  
**Quran refs:** The name Al-Jaleel comes from the Tirmidhi Hadith. The concept of His majesty (jalal) appears in Surah Ar-Rahman (55:27, 55:78): 'Blessed is the name of your Lord, the Owner of Majesty and Honor.'  

**Hadith:**  
> "Two words are light on the tongue, heavy on the scales, and beloved to Ar-Rahman: Subhanallahi wa bihamdih, Subhanallahil-Azeem." — Bukhari & Muslim. The glorification of Al-Azeem (the Magnificent) resonates with Al-Jaleel's majesty — these words are the tongue's acknowledgment of His incomparable greatness.

---

## 42. Al-Kareem — ٱلْكَرِيمُ

**Quran mentions:** 2  
**Quran refs:** Surah An-Naml (27:40): 'Whoever is grateful is grateful for his own soul.' Al-Infitar (82:6): 'O human, what has deceived you about Al-Kareem, your Lord?'  

**Hadith:**  
> "Our Lord is generous and loves generosity." — Tabarani

---

## 43. Ar-Raqeeb — ٱلْرَّقِيبُ

**Quran mentions:** 2  
**Quran refs:** Surah An-Nisa (4:1): 'Indeed Allah is ever, over you, Ar-Raqeeb.' Al-Ma'idah (5:117): 'You are the witness over all things.'  

**Hadith:**  
> The definition of ihsan (excellence) is: 'To worship Allah as though you see Him, and if you do not see Him, know that He sees you.' — Bukhari & Muslim

---

## 44. Al-Mujeeb — ٱلْمُجِيبُ

**Quran mentions:** 1  
**Quran refs:** Surah Hud (11:61): 'Indeed my Lord is near and responsive.' Surah Al-Baqarah (2:186): 'I respond to the call of the caller when he calls upon Me.'  

**Hadith:**  
> "Your Lord is modest and generous. He is shy to let His servant raise his hands to Him and return them empty." — Abu Dawud & Tirmidhi

---

## 45. Al-Waasi' — ٱلْوَاسِعُ

**Quran mentions:** 9  
**Quran refs:** 9 times. Al-Baqarah (2:115): 'To Allah belongs the east and west. Wherever you turn, there is the Face of Allah — indeed Allah is All-Encompassing, Knowing.' Also 2:268, 3:73.  

**Hadith:**  
> "Allah the Almighty said: 'If My servant intends to do a good deed and does not do it, I record one good deed for him. If he intends it and does it, I record ten to seven hundred times, even many more. If he intends an evil deed and does not do it, I record one good deed for him." — Bukhari & Muslim (Hadith Qudsi). Al-Wasi's encompassing mercy rewards even good intentions.

---

## 46. Al-Hakeem — ٱلْحَكِيمُ

**Quran mentions:** 97  
**Quran refs:** The most frequently occurring divine name — 97 times, mostly paired with Al-Aziz and Al-Aleem. Al-Baqarah (2:32), Al-Imran (3:6, 3:18), and throughout every juz.  

**Hadith:**  
> "No fatigue, nor disease, nor sorrow, nor sadness, nor hurt, nor distress befalls a Muslim — not even the prick of a thorn — except that Allah expiates some of his sins for it." — Bukhari & Muslim (from Abu Sa'id al-Khudri and Abu Hurayrah). Al-Hakeem's wisdom ensures that even the smallest suffering carries purpose.

---

## 47. Al-Wadood — ٱلْوَدُودُ

**Quran mentions:** 2  
**Quran refs:** Surah Hud (11:90): 'My Lord is Merciful and Wadood.' Al-Buruj (85:14): 'And He is Al-Ghafoor, Al-Wadood.'  

**Hadith:**  
> "When Allah loves a servant, He calls Jibril: 'I love so-and-so, so love him.' Then Jibril loves him and announces in the heavens: 'Allah loves so-and-so, so love him.' The inhabitants of the heavens love him. Then acceptance is placed for him on earth." — Bukhari & Muslim. Al-Wadood's love is active, spreading, and transformative.

---

## 48. Al-Majeed — ٱلْمَجِيدُ

**Quran mentions:** 2  
**Quran refs:** Surah Hud (11:73): 'Mercy of Allah and His blessings be upon you, O family of Ibrahim. Indeed He is Praiseworthy, Al-Majeed.' Al-Buruj (85:15): 'Owner of the Throne, Al-Majeed.'  

**Hadith:**  
> "Blessed are You, O Allah — Al-Majeed." — Part of the prophetic dua in the Abrahamic prayers (Ibrahim salutation in prayer).

---

## 49. Al-Baa'ith — ٱلْبَاعِثُ

**Quran mentions:** ?  
**Quran refs:** Al-Hajj (22:7): 'And that the Hour is coming — no doubt about it — and that Allah will resurrect those in the graves.' The concept of resurrection (ba'th) is one of the Quran's most repeated themes.  

**Hadith:**  
> "You will be resurrected barefoot, naked, and uncircumcised." — Bukhari & Muslim. Al-Ba'ith is the one who performs this ultimate act.

---

## 50. Ash-Shaheed — ٱلْشَّهِيدُ

**Quran mentions:** 18  
**Quran refs:** 18 times. Surah An-Nisa (4:33, 4:79), Al-Ma'idah (5:117), Al-An'am (6:19): 'Allah is witness between me and you.'  

**Hadith:**  
> "On the Day of Resurrection, Allah will bring His servant close and ask: 'Did I not honor you? Did I not give you a spouse, horses and cattle, and make you a leader?' He will say: 'Yes.' Then Allah will say: 'Did you think you would not meet Me?' He will say: 'No.' And Allah will say: 'I will not forget you today as you forgot meeting Me.'" — Muslim. Ash-Shaheed witnessed every moment — and testifies on that Day.

---

## 51. Al-Haqq — ٱلْحَقُّ

**Quran mentions:** 8  
**Quran refs:** 8 times as divine name. Surah Al-Hajj (22:6): 'That is because Allah is Al-Haqq and He gives life to the dead.' Yunus (10:32), Al-Kahf (18:44).  

**Hadith:**  
> "The truest word a poet has said is: 'All things except Allah are false.'" — Bukhari
